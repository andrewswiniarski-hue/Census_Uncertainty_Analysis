# WORKLOG.md — Team Work Log

A running record of the work done on this project: **who did it, when, and what came of it.** Newest entries go at the top.

**Why we keep this:** with the whole team working in one repo, this file is how we stay aware of each other's progress without reading every commit. It is also part of our defensibility story — when we assemble the final report and present to Census mentors, this is the paper trail of what was done, by whom, and what was found along the way.

---

## How to add an entry

When you finish a piece of work — a script, an analysis, a notebook, a document, a meeting deliverable — copy the template below to the **top of the Log section** (newest first) and fill it in. One entry per completed chunk of work; tiny fixes (typos, formatting) don't need one.

```markdown
### YYYY-MM-DD — Your Name — Short title of the work
- **Area:** ingestion | analysis | notebooks | docs | dashboard | project management
- **What was done:** 2–4 plain-English sentences a teammate on another workstream can follow.
- **Findings / decisions:** anything learned, decided, or surprising — data quirks especially. ("None" is fine.)
- **Files:** paths, notebook names, and/or commit hashes.
```

Ground rules:

- **Plain English first.** Write the summary so a teammate who wasn't there understands it without opening the code.
- **Surprises go here.** A weird distribution, an impossible value, a data quirk — log it even if it feels minor. (Also raise it with the team; see the "Data landmines" list in [HANDOFF.md](HANDOFF.md).)
- **Update the log in the same commit as the work** whenever possible, so the log and the repo never drift apart.
- New statistical terms still get defined in [docs/glossary.md](docs/glossary.md) — the log links to work, the glossary explains terms.

### 2026-07-16 — Andrew Swiniarski — EDA 04: privacy noise measured — DAS demonstration vs. published 2010 SF1 (Phase 1, Step 5)
- **Area:** notebooks / analysis
- **What was done:** Fourth EDA notebook, [notebooks/04-privacy-noise-das-demo.ipynb](notebooks/04-privacy-noise-das-demo.ipynb): joins the 2010 DHC demonstration counts (parsed by the new `analysis/dhc.py`) 1:1 against published 2010 SF1 at five levels (state → block, 177,940 geographies), and measures privacy noise = demonstration − published for total population and the Black 65+ subgroup. Headline method (lead-approved): **binned relative RMSE** — RMSE of errors per size bin ÷ mean published count, the empirical analog of a CV — with per-unit scatter as a supporting view (per-unit log-log fits would drop the many exactly-zero errors and bias the slope). Three mentor-ready charts saved to `data/processed/` (flagship: `eda04_noise_rmse_by_size.png`).
- **Findings / decisions:** (1) **The slope hypothesis is directionally confirmed**: within-level slopes for total population are −0.78 (block) / −0.85 (block group) / −0.90 (tract) — far steeper than the −0.5 sampling law (EDA 02) and near the −1 constant-absolute-noise ideal, so **the two dominant noise mechanisms follow measurably different laws** (honest caveat: absolute noise grows several-fold within a level, so exactly −1 it is not). (2) **Surprise — the block-group anomaly**: absolute noise by level is non-monotonic; block groups (RMSE 23.2 people) run ~9× tracts (2.5) and ~3.4× blocks (6.9); a same-size block group is ~12× noisier than a tract; for the P12B subgroup table, *county* is noisiest in absolute terms (34.6). Level — i.e., where the privacy-loss budget is spent — is a first-class reliability factor; mechanism hypothesis (off-spine block groups) logged as a mentor question, not asserted. (3) Practical: total-population privacy noise crosses the 0.30 caution convention only below ~21-person blocks and is negligible (≤0.2%) at tract/county scale; the Black 65+ subgroup sits at ~0.3–0.4 relative noise at the *median* tract (18 people) — same order as, but ~2–3× smaller than, its ACS sampling CV (0.886, EDA 02). (4) **Ghost/vanished blocks are a categorical artifact class**: 807 blocks gain 4,695 phantom residents, 427 lose all 1,154 of theirs. (5) Errors sum to exactly 0 at every level for total population (state invariant propagating down); the subgroup's statewide +16 error propagates identically — hierarchical consistency observed live.
- **Files:** notebooks/04-privacy-noise-das-demo.ipynb, docs/glossary.md (7 new entries), docs/data-dictionary.md (SF1 entry, demo-entry updates), README.md (Step 5 checkbox, two new mentor questions); charts at data/processed/eda04_*.png (local-only, regenerable)
